3 Lessons from 3 Years as a BSIT Student

Three years into my BSIT journey at Davao Oriental State University, I've picked up a few things that no syllabus could teach. Here are the three that matter most.

1. Build things, not just assignments

The biggest jump in my skills came when I started building projects outside of class requirements. The DOrSU Program Recommender, SweetWorks, Resibooth — none of these were homework. They were ideas I wanted to see through. Building real products forces you to deal with deployment, user feedback, and messy edge cases that assignments never cover.

2. Collaborate early and often

Some of my best work came from pairing with classmates. Talking through a design problem or debugging together always leads to better solutions faster. I've learned that asking for help isn't a weakness — it's how you ship better software.

3. Stay curious about the "why"

It's easy to follow tutorials and copy-paste code. But the real growth happens when you stop and ask why something works. Why does this CSS property behave this way? Why is this database query slow? That curiosity drives the kind of deep understanding that makes you a better developer long-term.

Three years down, one more to go. I'm excited to see what the next year brings.
